Coaching Philosophy

The Approach

Basketball has been a huge part of my life — as a player, as a coach, and as someone who has watched what performance culture can do for young people when it's built right. The lessons from the gym show up in my classroom every day, and the lessons from the classroom make me a better coach.

What I believe about coaching, at every level:

Culture First, Always

Programs that last are built on values, standards, and relationships — not just skill development. You have to build the environment before you build the players.

Design the Practice

Random reps don't make good players. Intentional, competitive, purposefully designed practice time is the difference between going through the motions and real improvement.

Develop the Whole Athlete

Basketball is a vehicle. The goal is young people who know how to work, compete, handle failure, lead, and perform under pressure. That skill set outlasts their last game.

Coaches Are Teachers

The best coaches I've learned from are great teachers. They can explain, demonstrate, adjust, and connect with their players as learners. That's the craft worth developing.
